Ōtākaro Living Laboratory

Explore our interactive map to discover five (of 31) rephotography sites within this dynamic and evolving river corridor.

The Ōtākaro Living Laboratory uses rephotography to capture changes over time in the post-earthquake landscape of Ōtautahi. Our rephotography project involves capturing repeat photographs of red zone views at different time intervals - replicating position, elevation, and orientation. Rephotography is our way of capturing a long-term record of visual change in the river corridor.

We aim to engage local (and international) communities in climate change awareness, by drawing attention to the significant environmental processes that you may not notice are changing...
